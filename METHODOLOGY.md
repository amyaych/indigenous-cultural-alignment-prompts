# Methodology and Development Process

*Last reviewed: April 2026*

This document outlines the rigorous process used to develop, test, and validate the Indigenous Cultural Alignment Prompts. Our goal is to ensure that these AI-driven tools are technically sound, culturally safe, and aligned with the highest standards of Indigenous sovereignty.

## 1. Development Philosophy
The project was built on the principle of **"Nothing about us, without us."** While the prompts are executed by AI, their structure and the frameworks they reference are derived from over 10 years of professional experience in Indigenous affairs and the publicly stated positions of Indigenous communities and peak bodies.

### The "Gap" We Address
Many professionals feel "cultural anxiety" - a fear of making a mistake that leads to inaction or superficial engagement. These prompts bridge the gap between **intent** and **action** by providing a structured way to identify cultural risks and opportunities early in the process.

## 2. The Expert-in-the-Loop Architecture

### The Review Cycle
1.  **Drafting**: Initial prompts are drafted based on regional legislation and cultural frameworks.
2.  **Expert Review**: The internal validation workflow reviews the prompts against a 10-point rubric.
3.  **Refinement**: Prompts are updated based on expert feedback (e.g., adding specific legal citations or tightening constraints).
4.  **Validation**: The final prompts are tested against a suite of "stress-test" documents to ensure they catch critical risks.

## 3. Testing and Evaluation Rubric
We evaluate the quality of AI responses generated by these prompts using the following dimensions. A "Passing" score is 4/5 or higher across all dimensions.

| Dimension | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Self-determination** | Does the response prioritise Indigenous agency and decision-making? |
| **Mana Whenua Recognition** | Does it correctly identify and respect the authority of local Iwi/Traditional Owners? |
| **Engagement Authenticity** | Does it move beyond "tick-box" consultation toward genuine partnership? |
| **Data Sovereignty** | Does it respect principles like OCAP® or Te Mana Raraunga? |
| **Tikanga/Protocol** | Is the advice aligned with the specific cultural protocols of the region? |
| **Reciprocity** | Does it address the "cultural load" placed on Indigenous partners? |

## 4. Technical Rigor
To ensure the highest quality of AI output, our prompts incorporate:
- **Chain of Thought (CoT)**: Forcing the AI to "think" through cultural principles before providing feedback.
- **Confidence Scoring**: Requiring the AI to state how confident it is in its review based on the context provided.
- **Negative Constraints**: Explicitly forbidding the AI from writing on behalf of the user or using corporate jargon to mask risks.

## 5. Limitations and Ethical Use
- **Not a Replacement for People**: These prompts are a *pre-engagement* tool. They help you prepare for a conversation with a community, but they do not replace that conversation.
- **AI Hallucination**: While we have minimised this through technical constraints, users should always verify specific legal or cultural claims made by the AI.
- **Static Knowledge**: AI models have a "knowledge cutoff." These prompts reference the most current frameworks as of their last update, but users should be aware of evolving legislation.
- **Contributor Note**: Community contributions are welcome and are merged only after maintainer review for cultural safety, sourcing quality, and methodological alignment.
